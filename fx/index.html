Below are our favourite forex books:


Forex Price Action Scalping

Few books have been published, if any, that take the matter of scalping to such a fine and detailed level as does Forex Price Action Scalping. Hundreds of setups, entries and exits (all to the pip) and price action principles are discussed in full detail


Encyclopedia of Chart Patterns

How to trade the significant events -- such as quarterly earnings announcements, retail sales, stock upgrades and downgrades -- that shape today?s trading and uses statistics to back up his approach.

How to Day Trade for a Living

For beginner traders, this book gives you an understanding of where to start, how to start, what to expect from day trading, and how to develop your strategy.


The Disciplined Trader

One of the first books on trading psychology. It teaches how to develop a winning mindset.


Day Trading Quickstart guide by Troy Noonan

An outline of the exact path that new forex traders should take including guidance on technical and fundamental analysis, interpreting charts, and mastering the psychology of successful forex traders.


Day trading by Justin Kuepper

This book focuses on helping beginners understand trading, charting, patterns, money management, and options.


High-Performance Trading by Steve Ward

A practical guide to enhancing your performance and psychology. A great book to coach you on your trading journey.


Trading in the zone by Mark Douglas

This book uncovers the underlying reasons for lack of consistency and helps traders overcome the ingrained mental habits that cost them money.


New Trader, Rich Trader by Steve Burns

The purpose of this book is to share with readers the principles of successful trading: methodology, risk management, and psychology. New traders usually learn these the hard way, by losing money.



Note: Simply reading these book, however, will not make you a profitable trader. Profit in trading does not come with reading a book or two or browsing online. It comes with practice, the right tools and software and appropriate ongoing education.
